Modeling an NBA pick swap as a Call Option

In finance, call option contract give the owner the right to purchase an underlying asset at some time in the future at a strike price set now, in exhange for an option premium or option price. If the underlying is worth more than the strike price money can be made, If however the strike is higher than the value of the underlying no purchase is made. If we replace the strike price with a strike asset the call option begins to look like an NBA draft pick swap.

A pick swap gives a team (say team A)the right to swap picks with another team (say team B). The swap is only going to be exercised if pick A is lower than pick B, as in the NBA a lower draft pick has higher value. Team A therefor buys the swap contract from Team B in exchange for some swap value. In the NBA this is normally occurs as part of a larger trade, where some players, draft picks and draft swaps are traded between teams.

Assigning a value or price to pick swaps is therefor important to teams deciding whether to make a trade or not. Call options
